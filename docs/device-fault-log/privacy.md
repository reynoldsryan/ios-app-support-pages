# Device Fault Log Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 9, 2026

Device Fault Log is a local-first iPhone app for recording household device fault history, status, notes, and support-call context.

## Data Collection

Device Fault Log does not collect personal data.

The app does not use accounts, tracking, advertising identifiers, third-party analytics SDKs, crash-reporting SDKs, backend services, cloud sync, paid APIs, AI services, OCR services, code-lookup databases, manual-search services, manufacturer integrations, repair marketplaces, or remote device-control services for its documented v1 scope.

## Data Stored On Your Device

The app stores the device names, categories, model or serial notes you choose to enter, fault labels/codes, status, notes, what you tried, local history, local support-summary text, optional local photo/manual labels, and Pro unlock state you create on your device using local iOS storage.

This information stays on your device and is not sent to us or shared with third parties by the app.

## Permissions

For its documented v1 scope, Device Fault Log does not require microphone, contacts, location, health, motion, Bluetooth, local network, local notifications, or tracking permissions. If future versions add photo import, photos you choose stay local unless you share them yourself through iOS share features.

## Purchases

Device Fault Log may offer an optional one-time Pro unlock through Apple In-App Purchase if a paid version is configured. Apple processes purchases through your App Store account. We do not receive your payment card details.

## No Diagnosis or Repair Advice

Device Fault Log helps you save what happened so you can reference it later. It does not diagnose faults, recommend repairs, interpret safety issues, or replace professional guidance.

## Data Deletion

Because app data is stored locally on your device, you can delete app data by deleting the app from your device.
