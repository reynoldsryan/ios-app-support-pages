# Return to Ship Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 9, 2026

Return to Ship is a local-first iPhone app for creating a manually entered cruise port-day return plan and checklist.

## Data Collection

Return to Ship does not collect personal data.

The app does not use accounts, tracking, advertising identifiers, third-party analytics SDKs, crash-reporting SDKs, backend services, cloud sync, paid APIs, maps, routing, live traffic, tender feeds, weather, port-status feeds, cruise-line sync, itinerary import, OCR, AI, location tracking, or external data sources for its documented v1 scope.

## Data Stored On Your Device

The app stores the all-aboard time, return buffer, optional last-tender time, pier or terminal note, ship-time note, transit note, checklist state, reboarded status, Pro unlock state, and local app history you create on your device using local iOS storage.

This information stays on your device and is not sent to us or shared with third parties by the app.

## Permissions

Return to Ship's documented v1 scope is local-first and does not require location, camera, photo library, microphone, contacts, calendar, health, motion, Bluetooth, local network, or tracking permissions.

If you choose to schedule local reminder notifications, iOS may ask for notification permission. Notification scheduling is handled on your device.

## Purchases

Return to Ship may offer an optional one-time Pro unlock through Apple In-App Purchase if a paid version is configured. Apple processes purchases through your App Store account. We do not receive your payment card details.

## Travel Boundary

Return to Ship provides a planning checklist based on times and notes you enter. It does not verify cruise-line, port, tender, traffic, customs, immigration, weather, or ship-time rules, and it does not guarantee you will make all-aboard.

## Data Deletion

Because app data is stored locally on your device, you can delete app data by deleting the app from your device.
